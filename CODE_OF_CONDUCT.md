# Code of Conduct

compas is built and used by people of many backgrounds and skill levels. This document sets the
ground rules for taking part — in issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other project space.

## Our intent

We want compas to be a welcoming, harassment-free place to learn, build, and ship. Technical
disagreement is healthy; disrespect is not. Assume good faith, be patient with newcomers, and keep
the focus on the work.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful and considerate in language and tone.
- Give and accept constructive feedback gracefully; critique ideas, not people.
- Respect differing viewpoints, experiences, and levels of expertise.
- Take responsibility for mistakes, apologize, and learn from them.
- Prioritize what is best for the project and the wider community.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind.
- Demeaning, insulting, or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Sexualized language or imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention.
- Publishing others' private information (such as a physical or email address) without explicit
  permission.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, or other conduct that is inappropriate in a professional
  setting.

## Scope

This applies in all project spaces — the repository, issues, pull requests, discussions — and when
an individual is representing the project in public.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it privately to the maintainers through
the repository's confidential reporting channel (GitHub's private report feature on the project, or
a direct message to a maintainer). Reports are handled discreetly. Please include what happened,
where, and any context or links that help us understand the situation.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying these standards and will take action they judge fair and
appropriate, which may range from a private warning to a temporary or permanent ban from project
spaces. Maintainers who do not act in good faith may face consequences decided by other maintainers.

This is a living document and may be refined as the community grows.
